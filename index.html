How to Reinstall Office or Microsoft 365 in Your Office Pre-installed PC

Your complete guide to reinstalling Office or Microsoft 365 quickly and securely.

Get Started with Microsoft Setup

If you purchased a new computer that came with Microsoft Office or Microsoft 365 already pre-installed, you may find yourself needing to reinstall it due to a system reset, software errors, or hardware changes. The process is straightforward if you follow the right steps, and in this guide, we’ll walk you through how to Reinstall Microsoft 365 Pre-installed or Reinstall Office 365 Pre-installed quickly and securely.

Many users assume that once the software is gone, they must purchase it again—but that’s not the case. If you have a valid subscription or license linked to your Microsoft account, you can reinstall the suite at no extra cost.

Why You May Need to Reinstall Microsoft 365 or Office 365

There are several reasons why you might have to Reinstall Microsoft 365 Pre-installed or Reinstall Office 365 Pre-installed:

  • Performing a Windows reset or system restore
  • Replacing your hard drive or upgrading your PC hardware
  • Experiencing software corruption or errors when opening apps
  • Resolving activation issues after signing into a different account
  • Moving your license to a new device after decommissioning the old one

No matter the reason, Microsoft makes it easy to reinstall as long as you have your account credentials.

Before You Start the Reinstallation

Before you jump into reinstalling, here’s what you need to have ready:

  • A Microsoft account with your Office or Microsoft 365 subscription linked
  • A stable internet connection for downloading the installer
  • Your product key (only if your Office license was not linked to your account)
  • Enough storage space on your PC for the installation

If your subscription is expired, you’ll need to renew it before proceeding.

How to Uninstall the Current Version (If Needed)

If you are reinstalling because of corrupted files or issues with the existing installation, it’s best to uninstall Office first:

  1. Open the Windows Start menu and select Settings.
  2. Go to Apps → Installed Apps (or Apps & Features on older Windows versions).
  3. Scroll to find Microsoft Office or Microsoft 365.
  4. Click Uninstall and follow the prompts.
  5. Restart your PC after the process is complete.

This ensures you start with a clean slate before reinstalling.

How to Reinstall Microsoft 365 or Office 365 Pre-installed

Here’s the step-by-step process to get your software back:

1. Sign into Your Microsoft Account

Open your browser and go to https://www.office.com.
Click Sign in and use the Microsoft account linked to your subscription.
If you purchased Office through a retailer, make sure you redeemed the product key before.

2. Locate Your Office Installation Option

Once signed in, you’ll see the Microsoft 365 Home Page.
Click on Install Office at the top right.
Choose Office 365 Apps (this includes Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, etc.).

3. Download the Installer

Your browser will prompt you to download the Office setup file.
Save it to your computer (usually in the Downloads folder).

4. Run the Installer

Double-click the downloaded file.
If prompted by User Account Control, click Yes.
The installer will start downloading and installing the full Office suite.

5. Wait for the Installation to Complete

The process can take a few minutes depending on your internet speed.
You’ll see an animation showing which apps are being installed.

6. Open Any Office App to Activate

After installation, open Word or Excel.
Sign in with your Microsoft account when prompted.
This will activate your Office suite.

7. Confirm Activation

Go to File → Account in any Office app.
Look for the message Product Activated.

What If You Don’t Have a Linked Microsoft Account?

If your pre-installed Office was activated with a product key only, you’ll need to:

  1. Find the product key from your purchase email or packaging.
  2. Go to https://setup.office.com.
  3. Redeem the key and link it to your Microsoft account for future reinstalls.

Once linked, you’ll never need the key again for that subscription—it will always be tied to your account.

Troubleshooting Reinstallation Problems

  • Can’t sign in? Reset your Microsoft account password at https://account.live.com/password/reset.
  • Installer stuck? Cancel, restart your PC, and try again.
  • Activation fails? Make sure your subscription is active and not expired.
  • Error messages? Use the Microsoft Support and Recovery Assistant tool.

Benefits of Reinstalling vs. Using Old Files

  • You get the latest software updates and features
  • Any corrupted or missing files are replaced
  • Security vulnerabilities are patched
  • Compatibility with the newest Windows updates is ensured

Maintaining Your Microsoft 365 After Reinstall

  • Enable automatic updates in Office settings
  • Sign in to your Microsoft account regularly to sync licenses
  • Back up your documents to OneDrive for easy recovery
  • Avoid installing from unverified third-party sources

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re setting up a new PC or fixing software issues, knowing how to Reinstall Microsoft 365 Pre-installed or Reinstall Office 365 Pre-installed saves you time and money. The process is simple as long as you have your Microsoft account ready and follow the official steps.
